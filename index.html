Thoughts in passing,
kept on purpose.

I'm a pastor at Yorkton Alliance Church in Saskatchewan. My work sits at an unusual crossroads — Anabaptist convictions, Wesleyan instincts, and a Korean voice speaking into a Canadian congregation.

This is where I keep what would otherwise drift away: a sermon written for one Sunday, a thought first posted at midnight, a chapter from the dissertation I'm finishing on Zwingli and Hubmaier. Some of it is in English. Some in Korean. All of it is unfinished in the way most honest theology is.
